Dr Mehrdad Asgari

Independent Researcher in Chemical Engineering, Materials Science, Porous Materials, Adsorption, and Data-Driven Modelling

I work at the interface of chemical engineering, materials science, and data-driven discovery, with interests spanning porous materials, adsorption and separations, metal-organic frameworks, interpretable machine learning, and structure–property relationships in complex materials.

My work aims to combine rigorous scientific understanding with practical relevance for both academia and industry.

Research Themes

My research covers porous materials, adsorption and separations, metal-organic frameworks, data-driven modelling in materials science, interpretable machine learning, and computational strategies for understanding structure–property relationships.

For Academic and Industrial Audiences

I am particularly interested in research questions that are scientifically rigorous, methodologically transparent, and practically meaningful, including problems relevant to sustainable materials, gas storage and separation, and data-enabled materials discovery.
